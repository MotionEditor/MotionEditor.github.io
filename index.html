Abstract

Existing diffusion-based video editing models have made gorgeous advances to edit attributes of source video over time but struggle to manipulate the motion information while preserving the original protagonist's appearance and background. In this paper, we propose MotionEditor, the first diffusion-based model to tackle video motion editing. MotionEditor plugs a novel temporal-latent-aware adapter into ControlNet for capturing temporal motion correspondences. However, adopting ControlNet to exploit the motion feature at the independent frame-wise level is challenging as the original ControlNet is derived from the image domain, which results in temporal inconsistency. Our adapter complements the ControlNet by leveraging mutual layered frame-wise representations and layered motion representations to transfer disentangled motion information across each frame. Further, we build up a two-branch architecture (a reconstruction branch and an editing branch) with an attention injection mechanism facilitating branch interaction. This mechanism enables the editing branch to query the key and value from the reconstruction branch in a decoupled manner, the editing branch to retain the original background and protagonist appearance. We also propose a skeleton alignment algorithm to counter the size and position disparities in the poses between the source and target videos. Extensive experiments demonstrate the promising motion editing ability of MotionEditor, both qualitatively and quantitatively.
